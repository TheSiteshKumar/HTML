HTML Entities

HTML entities are used to represent special characters in HTML. Here are some examples:

  • < represents the less than symbol: <
  • > represents the greater than symbol: >
  • & represents the ampersand symbol: &
  • " represents the double quote symbol: "
  • ' represents the single quote symbol: '
  • © represents the copyright symbol: ©
  • ® represents the registered trademark symbol: ®
  • € represents the Euro sign: €
  • ¥ represents the Yen sign: ¥
  • £ represents the Pound sign: £

You can use these entities to include special characters in your HTML content without causing any issues.

Example Usage

Here is an example of a paragraph containing several HTML entities:

<p>Tom & Jerry is a popular cartoon. It's loved by many. "Never underestimate the power of a mouse," they say. © 2024 Cartoon Network.</p>
